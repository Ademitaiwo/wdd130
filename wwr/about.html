History

Rafting, also known as whitewater rafting, is an adventurous water sport that involves navigating rivers and streams using inflatable rafts. Its origins date back to the early 19th century when explorers and traders used simple wooden rafts to transport goods across rivers. However, it wasnâ€™t until the mid-20th century that rafting evolved into a recreational sport. With the invention of durable rubber rafts after World War II, enthusiasts began to explore wild rivers for thrill and adventure, turning rafting into a popular outdoor activity across the world.

Over the years, rafting has grown into a well-organized sport with various difficulty levels, ranging from calm, scenic floats to adrenaline-pumping rapids. Professional rafting tours are now offered on rivers across many countries, promoting not only adventure tourism but also environmental awareness and river conservation. Today, whitewater rafting attracts millions of adventure seekers who crave the excitement of conquering challenging waters while enjoying the beauty of nature.
